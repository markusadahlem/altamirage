I’m currently visiting the [Mathematical Biosciences Institute](http://mbi.osu.edu/) at The Ohio State University. Last week, it started with a great workshop on [Mathematical Challenges in Neural Network Dynamics](http://mbi.osu.edu/2012/ws1description.html). (I actually heart for the first time [someone](http://neuroscience.nih.gov/lab.asp?org_id=522) mentioning [his blog](http://sciencehouse.wordpress.com/) in a talk.)

There will be soon the videos available, check out those from the last workshop: [Math Biology: Looking at the Future](http://beta.mbi.ohio-state.edu/video?view=events&id=166&item=Math%20Biology:%20Looking%20at%20the%20Future).

I will work here on ionic based models of neural activity, also sometimes referred to as second generation Hodgkin-Huxley models. There is a new postdoctoral fellow and another long-term visitor here at the MBI who work on related problems. So I am looking forward to  taking on this new endeavor in a math department.
